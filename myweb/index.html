INTRODUCTION

Intangible Cultural Heritage (ICH), as defined by UNESCO, encompasses practices, expressions, and skills that form the nonphysical intellectual wealth of a community. This includes folklore, customs, beliefs, and traditions, distinct from tangible heritage like buildings and monuments. Established to preserve and promote these cultural aspects, the UNESCO Convention for the Safeguarding of the Intangible Cultural Heritage was drafted in 2003.

Join us in exploring the richness of China's intangible cultural heritage and in carrying forward our cultural legacy!

Artists perform Yingge Dance at Burlington Arcade in London

LONDON, Feb. 11 (Xinhua) -- Yingge Dance, a form of folk dance popular in south China's Guangdong Province, was performed at Burlington Arcade in London on Saturday, offering a cultural feast for people there. The popular folk art form merging opera, dance, and martial arts dates back several hundred years.
